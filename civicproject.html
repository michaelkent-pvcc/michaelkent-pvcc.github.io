What is the Department of Homeland Security?

Established in 2002, the Department of Homeland Security is a unified collection of federal departments tasked with the protection of the United States. The umbrella of their responsibilities is wide and covers nearly every facet of national security. Such responsibilities range from the protection of key individuals, to Cybersecurity, to responding to natural disasters. As a result of these broad functions, the DHS has many component departments and nearly 300,000 employees. Continue reading for an overview of each of these component departments.

U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)

Leadership: Director Ur M. Jaddou

With over 19,000 employees and contractors, USCIS works to facilitate legal immigration from all around the world. Their core values state a commitment to both stakeholders and to those who seek citizenship.

United States Coast Guard (USCG)

Leadership: Admiral Linda L. Fagan

The US Coast Guard works to enforce maritime law, respond to disasters, protect the ecosystem, prevent marine casualties, protect trade routes, and defend against external or internal threats. The Coast Guard is often deployed around the globe to support naval operations elsewhere.

United States Customs and Border Protection (CBP)

Leadership: Commissioner Troy A. Miller

With over 60,000 employees, CBP works to secure US borders, prevent illegal trade, counter terrorism, illegal immigration, and facilitate lawful entry for trade or travel.

Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)

Leadership: Director Jen Easterly

CISA works to prevent cyberattacks, standardize, and reinforce Cybersecurity infrastructure across the nation.

Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)

Leadership: Administrator Deanne Criswell

With over 20,000 employees, FEMA works to rapidly respond to natural disasters. Their strategic plan states a focus on equity during emergency situations.

Federal Law Enforcement Training Center (FLETC)

Leadership: Director Benjamine C. Huffman

FLETC works to prepare and train federal law enforcement officers. Such officers range from AMTRAK police to ATF agents.

United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)

Leadership: Director Patrick J. Lechleitner

ICE works to enforce immigration laws and combat transnational crimes, often working with foreign agencies such as INTERPOL.

United States Secret Service (USSS)

Leadership: Director Kimberly A. Cheatle

The Secret Service works to protect key individuals, locations, events, and prevent financial crimes.

Transportation Security Administration (TSA)

Leadership: Administrator David P. Pekoske

With over 60,000 employees, the TSA works to protect public and private transportation system nationwide.

Management Directorate

Leadership: Unknown or Vacant

The Management Directorate is responsible for the distribution of resources between the other fifteen components of the DHS.

Science and Technology Directorate (S&T)

Leadership: Dimitri Kusnezov, Ph.D.

S&T is the research arm of the DHS, focusing on preparing the nation for technological changes, developing solutions for DHS components, and conducting scientific research.

Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction Office (CWMD)

Leadership: Assistant Secretary Mary Ellen Callahan

CWMD works to prevent the use of Weapons of Mass Destruction against the US domestically and abroad. CWMD often works with US allies to coordinate counter terrorism efforts.

Office of Intelligence and Analysis

Leadership: Secretary for Intelligence and Analysis Ken Wainstein

I&A works to rapidly assess and compile critical information for the deployment of appropriate DHS resources.

Office of Homeland Security Situational Awareness (OSA)

Leadership: Director Christopher Tomney

OSA works to coordinate between the components of the DHS, compiling reports and crucial information that require cross-component responses.

Ombudsman Offices

Split into two offices, The Office of the Immigration Detention Ombudsman works to resolve complaints and legal issues for detained immigrants, and The Office of the Citizenship and Immigration Services Ombudsman works to resolve complaints and legal issues for those immigrating to the US.
